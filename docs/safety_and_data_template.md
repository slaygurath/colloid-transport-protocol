# Laboratory Safety and Data Recording Template

## 1. Safety Precautions

### Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
- [ ] Lab coat (cotton or disposable)
- [ ] Safety goggles or face shield
- [ ] Nitrile gloves (double‑gloving recommended for handling concentrated contaminants)
- [ ] Closed‑toe shoes

### Chemical Handling
- **Synthetic fracking wastewater:** Contains high salt concentrations and trace metals (Ba, Sr).  
  - Handle in a fume hood if volatile organic compounds are present.
  - Avoid skin contact; rinse immediately with copious water if exposed.
- **Organic contaminants (PAHs, biocides, etc.):**  
  - Prepare stock solutions in a fume hood.
  - Use chemical‑resistant gloves (e.g., Silver Shield®).
  - Store in labeled, airtight containers away from light.
- **Acids/bases for pH adjustment:**  
  - Always add acid to water, never water to acid.
  - Use secondary containment for concentrated acids.

### Soil and Colloid Handling
- **Field soils:** May contain unknown pathogens or heavy metals.  
  - Wear gloves when processing; avoid generating aerosols.
  - Work on a disposable bench cover.
- **Colloid suspensions:**  
  - Centrifuge tubes must be balanced before spinning.
  - Use sealed containers to prevent spills.

### Waste Disposal
- Collect all contaminated soils, colloids, and wastewater in designated hazardous‑waste containers.
- Segregate organic waste (PAH‑spiked solutions) from inorganic waste (salt solutions).
- Label containers with date, contents, and hazard symbols.
- Consult your institution’s environmental health and safety office for disposal protocols.

### Emergency Procedures
- **Eye contact:** Flush with eyewash for at least 15 min, seek medical attention.
- **Skin contact:** Remove contaminated clothing, wash skin with soap and water.
- **Ingestion:** Do NOT induce vomiting; seek immediate medical help.
- **Spill:** Contain with absorbent pads, wear appropriate PPE, clean according to local spill‑response plan.
